1. PRODUCT VISION



The more we move together, the better for all.



















1.1 Where It Came From



Stretchy began as a social yoga community in Auckland, New Zealand in 2024. The idea was simple: the run club, but for yoga. People coming together to stretch bodies, minds & social circles.











Each session, a different teacher. 60 minutes of yoga, followed by a "social stretch" — coffee chats, wine hangs, beer and booch. It quickly became the highlight of attendees' weeks. Newbies and avid yogis, teachers, community managers, and everyone in between showed up. It worked.











Until it didn't.



















1.2 The Problem That Built the Platform



The model only worked en masse. It needed at least 15–20 people regularly showing up — not just for financial reasons, but for the social dynamic to actually land. The whole point was connection. You can't connect in a room of four.











The costs were real: paying teachers, space rental, prep time, marketing, subscriptions, platform fees, mat and prop hire. Stretchy often didn't break even — even while offering community pricing well below the $28–35 studio drop-in rate.











The energy input was overwhelming and unsustainable. One person holding everything together. No systems. No way to bring other teachers and coaches in without adding more weight to the same person.











There was a clear community need. The financial returns didn't convert. This is the common challenge across yoga, wellness, and the wider community movement space — the people most committed to building connection are often the least financially supported for doing it.











So the question became: how do you make movement more accessible and social for everyone, while making it financially viable for the people giving their time, expertise, and energy to hold space?



















1.3 The New Vision



A platform where Stretchy is the organisational and payment system — and vetted teachers, guides, coaches, and community builders host their own Stretchy meet-ups using the platform and its community.











Hosts set their financial target. The platform does the maths. The more people who join, the lower everyone's price. The host always hits their number. Stretchy keeps a flat fee per session.











If the minimum isn't reached: the host can override and run it anyway, or cancel — with no money taken from attendees. It also works for charity and donation-based sessions.











Stretchy becomes: a community movement platform with dynamic social pricing. Yoga, pilates, run clubs, sound baths, breathwork, HIIT — any movement that gets people in a room together. The pricing mechanic is the product. Community is the engine.



















1.4 Why Now



We are more fragmented as a society than at any point in recent memory. Small businesses are struggling — hospo, cafes, neighbourhood bars — the exact kinds of places where Stretchy social stretches happen. People are sitting more and moving less. The professional yoga and movement industry is hard to break into. The cost of living is relentless.











At the same time, AI has made this kind of tech accessible to build at a fraction of the historic cost. We don't need expensive custom builds that add to platform costs. We can build it, test it, and prove it — now.











The timing is right. The community need is real. The tech is available. And the model works.



















1.5 What the App Is



This app is the first digital expression of the Stretchy pricing engine — a live, real-time community pricing mechanic that makes showing up literally better for everyone. The more people who join, the less everyone pays. That's not a feature. That's the whole point.











The first format is yoga. The platform is movement. Yoga is the proving ground. The pricing mechanic, community model, and UX are designed to work across every format that gets bodies moving together.











This is also a live product experiment — weaving community design, IRL experience, and dynamic pricing into something that feels more like a cult event series than a booking app.

2.3 The Stretchy "S" as a Visual Device



The Stretchy logomark (the organic S-shape) is not just a logo — it's a living visual mechanic in the app.











Price drop visual: As more attendees join and the price drops, the S morphs, stretches, and shifts. At high price (few attendees), the S is tall and tight. As the price falls toward the floor, the S opens, relaxes, and becomes more fluid. This is the primary visual metaphor for the pricing engine.











Implementation options:











SVG path morphing between 3–5 keyframe states (starting price → mid → floor)



CSS animation triggered by currentSpots / maxSpots ratio



Could be used as a progress indicator on session cards in place of a standard progress bar











Price display: Consider using the S shape as a visual container for the price number — the price sits inside or beside the S, which transforms as the price drops.











Loading / transition states: The S can animate (stretch, bounce, flow) during loading screens, transitions between states.











Session card badge: Small S mark in the corner of each card, colour-coded by status (pink = open, yellow = filling, orange = almost full, hot-pink = confirmed).

4. THE PRICING ENGINE



This is the heart of the platform. Keep it this simple everywhere it's explained.





4.1 How it works



The host sets two numbers. That's it.











Financial target — what they need to earn (venue + costs + time)



Minimum spots — the smallest group needed for the session to run











The platform calculates:











(Target + $20 + GST) ÷ minimum spots = Starting price











The starting price is the most anyone will ever pay. Every new person who joins drops the price for everyone.





4.2 The Five Phases





Phase 1 — HOLD (below minimum)



Session open. Attendees hold a spot. No charge.



Price shown = starting price (ceiling, never exceeded)



Progress bar shows: "X of [minimum] spots held"



Price holds steady until the minimum is reached — every hold is a step closer





Phase 1b — HOLD (minimum reached — LIVE DROP begins) ⚡



Minimum holds reached. Price starts dropping immediately — before the 36hr check.



Every new hold above the minimum drops the price for all existing holders in real time.



Session is still subject to 36hr confirmation, but the price is already moving.



Holders can still cancel before the 36hr check. If a cancellation drops the count back below minimum, price recalculates upward accordingly.



Notification to all holders: "[Session] just hit its minimum — the price started dropping. Every new person brings it down further. Share it."



Progress bar shifts to live price display: current price + "still dropping"











Engineering note: This requires real-time price recalculation during the hold phase, not just post-confirmation. The formula (Target + $20 + GST) ÷ current hold count runs live once hold count ≥ minimum. Cancel logic must recalculate upward if count drops. Supabase real-time subscriptions handle this — every hold/cancel triggers a recalculate + push notification to all holders.





Phase 2 — 36-HOUR CHECK



36 hours before the session:











Minimum met → Session CONFIRMED. All holders locked in. No more cancellations.



Minimum not met → Session CANCELLED. All holds released. No charges. Transparent comms to all.



Edge case: If session is posted with <36hrs notice, system skips to 2hr lock-in.





Phase 3 — LIVE DROP (post-confirmation, continues from Phase 1b)



Session confirmed. No cancellations.



New joiners still welcome up to venue capacity. Price keeps dropping.



Existing holders notified of each price drop.



Scarcity trigger at 80% full: Session card shows "⚡ Filling fast". Holders get: "[Session] is nearly full — share it to drop the price further and grab those last spots."





Phase 4 — LOCK-IN



2 hours before: price freezes for everyone.



Stripe charges all confirmed holders at the final locked price.



Receipt sent to each attendee.





4.3 Host Pricing Prompt



When a host sets up a session, the platform surfaces a quiet market reference:











"For a 60-min casual vinyasa in Auckland, fair market range is $25–35. At your $200 target with 8 minimum spots, your starting price is $28. Feels about right."











This is a reference, not a rule. Hosts can override at any time — one tap, no friction, no explanation needed. Special events, exclusive venues, longer formats, charity sessions — host has full control.

Payment & Cancellation — What You Need to Know











When you hold a spot, we collect your payment details via Stripe. You are not charged at this point.

Host Responsibilities — What You're Agreeing To

About Page



Hero: "A social movement."











Subhead: "The more we move together, the better for all."











Body:











Stretchy is an IRL movement community for people who want to move — without the membership, the markup, or the loneliness of doing it alone.











We connect people through vetted, local sessions. Yoga, run clubs, breathwork, sound baths, pilates, dance. Led by people who know their practice and their community. Priced to get better the more people show up.











Here's the thing: movement is better together. And fairer together, too. The more people who join a Stretchy session, the less everyone pays. That's not a gimmick — it's the whole point. Community is the engine.











How it started:











Stretchy began in Auckland in 2024 as a social yoga club. The idea was simple: the run club, but for yoga. Different teacher each week. 60 minutes of movement, then a "social stretch" — coffee, chats, a cold one, good company.











It became the highlight of people's weeks, fast. Beginners and yoga teachers, community managers and total newcomers — all showing up, moving together, staying for the social stretch.











But the model only worked when enough people showed up. The costs were real — teachers, venues, prep time, equipment — and when numbers dropped, it stopped being viable. A common problem in the yoga, wellness, and community space. The people most committed to building connection are often the least financially supported for doing it.











So we built the fix.











Why Stretchy exists:











Fitness got expensive. Classes got lonely. Small businesses — the cafes, bars, and community spaces where people actually connect — are struggling. And the gap between a fair price and feeling like you genuinely belong somewhere kept getting wider.











We started in Tāmaki Makaurau with a simple idea: what if the price of a class dropped as more people joined? What if your neighbours, your workmates, the person you always see at the park were all moving together — and all paying less because of it?











That's Stretchy. A community that literally gets better — and cheaper — the more people are in it. The host always hits their number. The community always gets a fair price. And the more people who show up, the better it is for everyone in the room.











Why join:











Because movement is better when it's shared. Because $28 is already fair — and it only gets better from there. Because the Social Stretch after (coffee, a drink, a chat, someone new) is sometimes the best part. Because walking into a room of people who all chose to show up and connect — that's not a gym class. That's community.











Stretchy in Auckland — Stretchy Yoga IRL:











Stretchy started with yoga. In Auckland. In community halls, local parks, and borrowed spaces. Before the platform, before the app, there were just people who wanted to move — and a host with a mat and a belief that movement should be for everyone.











Stretchy Yoga IRL was the first expression of what we're building. The platform is how we bring that feeling to more people, more places, more formats.











Auckland is where we started. Other cities are coming. Join the waitlist and we'll bring Stretchy to you.











Why now:











We are more fragmented than ever. People are sitting more and moving less. The cost of living is brutal. And the small businesses that used to be our third spaces — the neighbourhood cafes, bars, and community spots — are under real pressure.











Stretchy is built for this moment. More accessible movement. More reasons to show up together. More people in the cafes and bars and community halls that need them.











The tech that makes this possible is finally accessible. So we built it.

Stretchy works because of the people around the table. We're building something that needs community at every level — not just attendees and hosts, but the venues, businesses, and brands that believe in what fair, human movement looks like.
